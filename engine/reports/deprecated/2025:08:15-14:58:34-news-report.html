What Happened

On August 15, 2025, U.S. President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin convened at Joint Base Elmendorf–Richardson in Anchorage, Alaska, for a pivotal summit focused on the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict. This meeting marks the first between the two leaders since Trump's re-election in 2024 and the first as sitting presidents since their 2019 meeting in Osaka. The primary agenda includes negotiating a ceasefire in Ukraine and addressing escalating nuclear tensions. Notably, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy was not present at the summit, raising questions about the inclusivity of the peace process. (en.wikipedia.org)
